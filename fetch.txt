title=\"Cardiff\">Cardiff</a>, Wales. They entered the <a href=\"/wiki/Southern_Football_League\" title=\"Southern Football League\">Southern Football League</a> in 1910 and joined the <a href=\"/wiki/English_Football_League\" title=\"English Football League\">English Football League</a> (EFL) in 1920. In <a href=\"/wiki/1927_FA_Cup_Final\" title=\"1927 FA Cup Final\">1927</a> they became the only team from outside England to have won the <a href=\"/wiki/FA_Cup\" title=\"FA Cup\">FA Cup</a>. They have won the <a href=\"/wiki/Welsh_Cup\" title=\"Welsh Cup\">Welsh Cup</a> 22 times, making them the second-best performers in the competition's history behind <a href=\"/wiki/Wrexham_A.F.C.\" title=\"Wrexham A.F.C.\">Wrexham</a>. <a href=\"/wiki/Ninian_Park\" title=\"Ninian Park\">Ninian Park</a> <i>(grandstand pictured)</i> was their home ground for 99 years, until they moved into the <a href=\"/wiki/Cardiff_City_Stadium\" title=\"Cardiff City Stadium\">Cardiff City Stadium</a> in 2009. They have long-standing rivalries with two nearby clubs, the <a href=\"/wiki/South_Wales_derby\" title=\"South Wales derby\">South Wales derby</a> with <a href=\"/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C.\" title=\"Swansea City A.F.C.\">Swansea City</a> and the <a href=\"/wiki/Severnside_derby\" title=\"Severnside derby\">Severnside derby</a> with <a href=\"/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C.\" title=\"Bristol City F.C.\">Bristol City</a>. The club's top goalscorer is <a href=\"/wiki/Len_Davies\" title=\"Len Davies\">Len Davies</a> with 179 goals. certification\">certification</a> levels set up by the <a href=\"/wiki/Uniunea_Produc%C4%83torilor_de_Fonograme_din_Rom%C3%A2nia\" title=\"Uniunea Producătorilor de Fonograme din România\">Uniunea Producătorilor de Fonograme din România</a> (UPFR). When considering an album's certification level, the UPFR considers both its unit sales and its sales price. From lowest to highest, the certifications that have been awarded over the years are: gold (ranging from 2,000 to 100,000 units sold), double gold, platinum (10,000 to 250,000 units), double platinum (100,000 units), triple platinum, quadruple platinum, diamond (60,000 units) and triple diamond (150,000 units). The first known release to be certified in Romania was the album <i>Party in Transylvania</i> by Romanian singers Marius and Nadia Dragomir, which received a gold certification in 1996. Romania's highest certified record is <i>La vârsta de trei ani</i> by Moldovan singer <a href=\"/wiki/Cleopatra_Stratan\" title=\"Cleopatra Stratan\">Cleopatra Stratan</a> <i>(pictured)</i>, which was awarded a triple diamond certification in 2006 for selling 150,000 units. The only other artists to receive a diamond award are <a href=\"/wiki/Andra_(singer)\" title=\"Andra (singer)\">Andra</a> and Sandel Mihai, for selling 60,000 copies of their 2007 album <i>De la frate la soră</i>.
